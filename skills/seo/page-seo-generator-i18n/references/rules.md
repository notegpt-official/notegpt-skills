## Writing Conventions by Language

> **Core Philosophy**: Use English content as the information source and page structure reference. ### Chinese (zh) — Chinese Page Writing Rules

Chinese pages are not translations of English pages — they are SEO content re-created for Chinese-speaking users. Based on the target keyword and user search intent, reorganize content according to Chinese reading habits, ensuring it reads like a native Chinese website, not a translation.

---

#### 1. Use Natural Chinese Expressions, Avoid Literal Translation

- Do not translate English content sentence by sentence
- Do not preserve English sentence structures
- Reorganize sentences, paragraphs, and content order according to Chinese conventions
- Prioritize Chinese reading fluency over exact correspondence with the English original

**Sentence structure requirements**:
- 🔴 **Prefer short sentences**: Keep each sentence to 15–25 characters; break long sentences into shorter ones
- 🔴 **Use parallel structures**: Use parallelism to enhance rhythm for similar points (e.g. `上传即处理、点击即出图、下载即使用`)
- 🔴 **Prefer affirmative statements**: Instead of double negatives like "不是不能" or "未必不好", use direct expressions like "可以", "能够", "帮你"
- Keep paragraphs to 2–3 sentences; large blocks of text will be skipped

**Avoid**:
- ❌ Literal translation: `这是一个可以帮助你提升图片质量的在线工具`
- ✅ Natural Chinese: `在线提升图片质量，上传就能用`
- ❌ Long attributive stacking: `这是一个基于先进人工智能深度学习算法的…`
- ✅ Short sentences: `基于AI深度学习算法。上传图片，自动优化。`
- ❌ Filler phrases: `众所周知、随着时代发展、在当今社会`
- ❌ Embedding English CTR words in Chinese text (e.g. "Free")

---

#### 2. Maintain a Professional but Readable Product Page Tone

Chinese pages should strike a balance between "overly formal/written" and "overly colloquial" — natural, clear, easy to understand, while maintaining a product introduction's sense of professionalism. Slightly written but not stiff; slightly conversational but not casual.

**Overly written** ❌:
> 利用人工智能技术实现智能化图像处理能力。

**Overly colloquial** ❌:
> 快来试试看，马上帮你换个造型！

**Recommended** ✅:
> 上传图片后，AI 可以快速生成自然的换装效果，无需复杂操作。

**🔴 Colloquial boundary**: The following expressions are too colloquial for SEO product pages and should be avoided:
- ❌ Tone particles: `吧` `呢` `嘛` `哦` `啦` (e.g. "试试看吧" "超好用哦")
- ❌ Colloquial verbs: `撑着` `扛着` `搞定` `甩过去` `扛不住`
- ❌ Overly intimate expressions: `真心推荐` `超好用` `太香了` `绝了`
- ✅ Corresponding replacements: `支撑` → `支持业务运转`, `搞定` → `完成`, `甩过去` → `发送`, `真心推荐` → `推荐`

**Tone checklist**:
- Get straight to the point; state the core value in the first sentence
- No detours, no preamble; skip phrases like "随着互联网的发展…"
- Data-driven feel: use numbers whenever possible ("3秒出图" "清晰度提升4倍")
- Professional but not stiff: clear information, direct expression; avoid colloquial particles and internet slang

---

#### 3. Prioritize User Value Over Feature Descriptions

Shift content focus from "what features the product has" to "what the user gains."

**Don't just list features** ❌:
> 支持 AI 图片生成、背景修改和图像编辑。

**Express value instead** ✅:
> 使用 AI 图片工具，无需专业设计经验，也能快速制作高质量视觉内容。

**Emphasize**: time savings, lower operational difficulty, improved efficiency, reduced production costs, helping users complete specific tasks.

---

#### 4. Avoid Exaggerated Marketing Language

Chinese users prefer credible, specific information. Unless backed by real data, avoid:

❌ 全球领先、顶级技术、革命性创新、颠覆行业、最强大的、惊艳体验

✅ 简单易用、快速生成、自然效果、高质量输出、提升工作效率

---

#### 5. Adapt Content to Chinese User Needs

Do not strictly confine yourself to the English page's information scope. You may supplement information, adjust priorities, and optimize examples based on what Chinese users care about.

**Chinese users typically pay more attention to**:
- Whether it's free
- Whether download is required
- Whether operation is simple
- Whether it's suitable for beginners

**Must not do**: Add non-existent product features or modify product facts.

---

#### 6. CTAs That Fit Chinese Expression Conventions

Chinese CTAs should be concise and clear. Avoid overly salesy expressions and English-literal-translation-style CTAs.

✅ Recommended: `免费生成图片` `上传图片开始创作` `立即体验` `开始使用`
❌ Avoid: `不要错过改变未来的 AI 工具` `立即开启你的 AI 革命`

---

#### 7. Maintain Terminology Consistency

- Keep product feature names consistent throughout the text
- On first occurrence, use "Chinese + English name"; thereafter, use the Chinese term consistently
- Avoid using different names for the same feature in different places

**Correct** ✅: Use `AI样机生成器` consistently throughout
**Error** ❌: `AI样机生成器` in one place, then `AI商品详情工具` or `AI产品图片工具` elsewhere

---

#### CTR Vocabulary (integrate naturally, do not stuff)

免费、在线、一键、不用下载、不用注册、秒出图、自动、智能、高清、无损

### Japanese (ja) — Japanese Page Writing Rules

Japanese pages are not direct translations of English pages — they are SEO content re-created for Japanese users. Based on the target keyword, page positioning, and search intent, localize according to Japanese users' reading habits, expression styles, and cultural preferences.

---

#### 1. Prioritize Natural Japanese Expression, Avoid Literal Translation

- Do not translate English content word by word
- Do not preserve English syntactic structures
- Reorganize sentences and paragraphs according to Japanese expression conventions
- Prioritize natural fluency over consistency with English sentence structure

**Avoid**:
- Unnatural English-influenced Japanese
- Excessively long sentences
- Unnatural stacking of loanwords

**Recommended**:
- Concise, natural Japanese
- Expressions commonly seen on Japanese websites
- Sentence structures that match reader habits

---

#### 2. Maintain a Polite, Professional, and Easy-to-Understand Tone

Japanese business websites typically favor a polite, stable, and trustworthy impression, avoiding excessive sales emphasis.

🔴 **Use です・ます体 consistently throughout the entire text**. Never mix in である調 or だ体.
- Vary sentence endings appropriately: use 〜できます, 〜です, 〜ます, 〜しましょう in moderation to avoid monotony
- Do not be excessively humble (repeating 〜させていただきます sounds unnatural)

**Recommended expressions**:
- 〜できます
- 〜を利用できます
- 〜をサポートします
- 〜に役立ちます
- 〜を実現します

**Good** ✅:
> AIを使って、写真を簡単に編集できます。

**Bad** ❌ (overly formal with a translated feel):
> 革新的なAIテクノロジーによって、ユーザーは高度な画像変換体験を享受できます。

---

#### 3. Avoid Exaggerated Marketing Language

Japanese users are sensitive to excessive marketing. Unless there is clear evidence, avoid:

❌ 世界最高、業界最先端、革新的、圧倒的、驚異的、最強

✅ 簡単、便利、自然な仕上がり、高品質、効率的、初心者でも使いやすい

---

#### 4. Emphasize Reassurance and Ease of Use

Key factors Japanese users consider when choosing online tools:

- Is operation simple?
- Is it trustworthy?
- Is it suitable for beginners?
- Does it save time?

**Elements that can be naturally emphasized** (based on actual features):
- 簡単な操作
- 初心者でも使いやすい
- 面倒な作業を減らせる
- 短時間で作成できる

**🔴 Do not fabricate claims**: Do not state security certifications, user counts, corporate endorsements, or other information that is not factual.

---

#### 5. Control Sentence Length for Web Readability

While Japanese can support long sentences, web content prioritizes readability above all.

🔴 **Actively use bullet points (箇条書き)**: Organize features, steps, and benefits with bullet points to reduce reading fatigue.
- One point per sentence
- Avoid consecutive modifiers
- Add line breaks appropriately
- Break paragraphs at 2–3 sentences; avoid consecutive long sentences

**Bad** ❌:
> 最新の人工知能技術を活用した高度な画像生成システムにより複雑な編集作業を必要とせず……

**Good** ✅:
> 最新のAI技術で画像を生成できます。複雑な編集作業は必要ありません。

---

#### 6. Structure Content for Logical Flow and Trustworthiness

🔴 **Write in the order: conclusion → reasoning → concrete examples**. Japanese readers find this structure reassuring.
- Use connectives like なぜなら, 例えば, 具体的には to make logical progression explicit
- Add specificity with numbers and data (処理時間は約3秒, 最大4倍まで拡大可能)
- Avoid exaggerated expressions (驚くほど, 信じられない); write based on facts

---

#### 7. Appropriately Leverage Expressions Japanese Users Care About

Based on the product's actual features, the following may be emphasized:

- 無料で試せる
- 登録不要
- 簡単操作
- オンラインで利用可能
- ダウンロード不要

**However, only mention these if the product actually supports them.**

---

#### 8. Use Gentle, Guiding CTA Expressions

Japanese page CTAs generally use softer expressions than English ones.

**Recommended** ✅:
- `今すぐ試してみる`
- `無料で体験する`
- `AI画像を作成する`
- `画像をアップロードして始める`

**Avoid** ❌:
> 今すぐ革命的なAI体験を始めましょう！

**Good** ✅:
> 写真をアップロードして、簡単にAI画像を作成できます。

---

#### 9. Maintain Consistent Product Terminology

- Keep feature names consistent throughout the entire text
- On first occurrence: "English + Japanese explanation"; thereafter, unify to the Japanese term
- Do not use different names for the same feature (except for SEO keyword coverage purposes)

**Good**:
First occurrence: `AI Image Generator（AI画像生成ツール）`
Thereafter: `AI画像生成ツール`

**Bad** ❌: Mixing `AI画像メーカー`, `AI画像作成ツール`, `AI画像生成サービス` for the same feature.

---

#### Katakana Usage

Foreign and technical terms should be written in katakana:
- ツール、アップスケーラー、エンハンサー、ジェネレーター、エディター、リムーバー
- オンライン、ブラウザ、ダウンロード、アップロード、クリック
- 🔴 Add brief explanations for technical terms on first occurrence (e.g. `アップスケーラー（画像を高解像度化する機能）`)

---

#### CTR Words (integrate naturally)

無料、登録不要、オンライン、ブラウザ完結、簡単3ステップ、ワンクリック、高速処理、高画質

---

#### Anti-patterns (Forbidden Patterns)

- ❌ Mixing polite and plain forms: `これは便利です。誰でも使える。` → `これは便利です。誰でも使えます。`
- ❌ English word order forced into Japanese: `これは素晴らしいですツール` → `これは素晴らしいツールです`
- ❌ Inserting English CTR words into Japanese text ("Free" → 「無料」)
- ❌ Excessive keigo: 「ご利用いただけますでしょうか」(double keigo) → 「ご利用いただけます」
- ❌ Unsubstantiated superlatives: 「最高の」「世界一」→ differentiate with specific features

## Anchor Text Links

Each page must include **5 anchor text links**:

### Self-links (3 — in Col 3/4/5 body text)

In Col 3, 4, 5 body text, the **first natural occurrence** of the core keyword becomes a clickable link to the current page.

```
[Core Keyword](https://your-domain.com/{lang}/{path})
```

### Cross-links (2 — at the end of Col 3 body text)

The user provides 2 cross-feature links each time. Integrate them naturally at the end of the Col 3 body text as an extension of the feature scenario — not as a forced "try our other tools" pitch. Freely polish the transition based on the relationship between the current feature and the cross-features.

🔴 **Cross-links follow the body text directly — no line break, no separate paragraph**. They should flow as a natural extension, immediately after the period of the last sentence in the Col 3 body.

- User provides: English feature name + English URL
- Auto-localize: Translate feature name to Chinese/Japanese, prefix URL with `/zh-CN/` or `/ja/`

**Example (Chinese — mockup generator + product photography / background changer)**:
```
…一个工具即可完成。需要实物拍摄或替换背景时，搭配[AI产品摄影](https://your-domain.com/zh-CN/ai-product-photography)和[AI背景替换](https://your-domain.com/zh-CN/ai-background-changer)使用效果更完整。
```

**Example (Japanese — same scenario)**:
```
…ブラウザだけで簡単に作成できます。なお、実物の商品撮影や背景の差し替えには、[AI商品撮影](https://your-domain.com/ja/ai-product-photography)や[AI背景変更](https://your-domain.com/ja/ai-background-changer)もあわせてご活用いただけます。
```

- H1: from TDK Title (user-provided)

- H2 format: question or statement form in target language
  - zh: `什么是[Core Keyword]？`
  - ja: `[Core Keyword]とは？`
- Body: language-specific limit, embed core keyword naturally
- Explain what the tool is, what it does, primary use cases

### Column 4: Recommendation
- H2 format: benefit-oriented with core keyword
  - zh: `为什么选择[Core Keyword]？`
  - ja: `[Core Keyword]を選ぶ理由`
- Body: language-specific limit
- Highlight unique value, competitive advantages, what makes it stand out

### Column 5: Pain Points
- H2: language-specific limit
  - zh: e.g. `您是否遇到这些问题？`
  - ja: e.g. `こんなお悩みはありませんか？`
- Body: language-specific limit
- MUST reflect real user pain points relevant to the target language market
- Address frustrations that the tool solves

### Column 6: Feature Highlights
- H2: language-specific limit
- P (Intro): brief intro sentence before highlights
- 3 highlights, each with:
  - H3: ≤15 chars (zh) / ≤20 chars (ja)
  - P: strictly within language-specific limits
- Each highlight describes one key feature/benefit

### Column 7: How to Use / Tutorial
- H2: language-specific limit
  - zh: `如何使用[Core Keyword]`
  - ja: `[Core Keyword]の使い方`
- 3 steps, each with:
  - H3: ≤15 chars (zh) / ≤20 chars (ja)
  - P: strictly within language-specific limits
- Default structure: Upload → Generate → Download

### Column 8: CTA
- H2: language-specific limit, use long-tail keyword
- P: language-specific limit
- Button text: ~8 chars (zh), ~15 chars (ja)
  - zh: `免费开始使用` / `立即体验`
  - ja: `無料で始める` / `今すぐ試す`

### Column 9: User Reviews
- 6 reviews, each within language-specific limits
- Each MUST contain core keyword
- Format: name initial + identity + review text
- H2 format:
  - zh: `用户如何评价[Core Keyword]`
  - ja: `[Core Keyword]のユーザーレビュー`
- Reviews should sound like real users from the target language market
- Vary identity types (designer, student, marketer, photographer, etc.)

### Column 10: FAQ
- 6 Q&A pairs
- 🔴 **Keyword in BOTH Q and A** — every Q and every A must embed core keyword
- Answers: strictly within language-specific limits
- 🔴 **No overlap with Col 3-8**: FAQ must cover complementary topics not addressed earlier:
  - Licensing/rights, privacy, output specs, compatibility, usage limits
  - Batch generation, refunds, supported formats, data security
  - Do NOT repeat: product definition (Col 3), reasons to choose (Col 4), pain points (Col 5), features (Col 6), tutorial steps (Col 7), CTA pitch (Col 8)

## Writing Style — Universal Principles

Use English content as the information baseline; localize the expression. Every rule serves the goal of "generating native SEO pages for the target language market."

1. **Information consistency, expression localization**: Product features, technical parameters, and core selling points must align with the English page; sentence structures, tone, examples, and CTAs should be independently crafted per target language culture. The English page serves as an information source and structural reference — not as copy source material.
2. **Cultural adaptation first**: Different language markets have different reading habits, trust-building methods, and decision-making paths. Follow the per-language writing conventions in `references/rules.md`.
3. **Search engine + human reader dual-friendliness**: Embed keywords naturally; do not sacrifice readability for SEO, nor sacrifice search visibility for fluency.
4. **Trustworthiness over sales pressure**: Do not use exaggerated vocabulary, fake urgency, or low-quality clickbait. Build trust through specific data and clear logic.
5. **Scannable paragraphs**: Body text no more than 3 sentences per paragraph; front-load key information; use lists and short paragraphs to reduce cognitive load.
6. 🔴 **Pricing accuracy**: Unless the product is completely free, never claim "completely free", "no subscription required", or "no fees of any kind". Use phrasing such as "free to use", "free quota available", "無料で利用可能", implying potential usage limits or paid tiers.

## Verification (Internal, Not in Output)

🔴 **Python `len()` verification is MANDATORY before writing the output file.** Never trust eyeball estimates.

Run verification on ALL character-critical fields per language-specific limits internally, but do NOT include the verification report in the output `.md` file.

```bash
python3 -c "
# Language: zh or ja — adjust limits accordingly
print('=== Character Count Verification ===')
print(f'Hero Description: {len(\"<content>\")} chars (limit: <limit>)')
print(f'H2-Col3: {len(\"<content>\")} chars (limit: <limit>)')
print(f'Body-Col3: {len(\"<content>\")} chars (limit: <limit>)')
# ... repeat for all fields
print()
print('=== Brand Word Count ===')
print(f'Brand word occurrences: {\"<full_content>\".count(\"<brand>\")}')
"
```

## Output Delivery

- **Write to `.md` file** — do NOT output content inline in chat
- File name: `SEO-[keyword]-[brand]-[lang].md`
- All 10 columns in `| Section | Content |` table format (output in target language)
- **Output file contains ONLY the 10 content columns** — no Schema Markup, no verification report
- Clean output: no character count tails `(XXc)`, no stray `|`, no artifacts
- Content cells: plain text, no bold/italic formatting
